Q2. Explain the advantage and disadvantages of statute law as a source of law in Kenya.
Statute refers to law which is made by a body specifically constituted for that purpose.
Advantages of statute law

  1. It is democratic in nature:

    2. It is democratic in the sense that it reflects the wishes of the majority as to what the law should be. This is because it is made by parliament which consists of representatives of the people.

  2. Resolution of legal problems:

    3. Enables parliament to find legal solutions to any problem the country may face by passing new laws or amending existing ones.

  3. Dynamic:

    4. It is dynamic as it can be changed depending on the changes in the society

  4. Flexible

    5. Can be applied to different situations in a flexible manner as determined by the courts in a particular situation.

  5. Publication:

    6. The requirement that an Act of parliament must be publicized gives Kenyans the opportunity to debate the proposed law from the moment they become aware of it after its publication in the Kenya Gazette.

Disadvantages of statute law

  1. Difficult to reappeal/ cancel:

    2. It is difficult to reappeal/ cancel an Act of parliament once it has been formed.

  2. Misunderstanding of technical bills:

    3. In case of technical bills, MPs may not fully understand the implications of such bills and may still pass them into law hence leading to unfair laws..

  3. Dictatorship:

    4. May lead to dictatorship of the majority against the minority whose views may not be considered.

  4. Inappropriate way of forming laws

    5. 4. It is not the appropriate way of forming a law which is needed urgently because of the long procedure of forming a statute plus the fact that parliament is not always in session.
