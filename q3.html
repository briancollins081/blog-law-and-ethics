Q3. Distinguish between the following terms.
  1. Public and private law,
  2. Law and morality,
  3. Substantive and procedural law

Public law may be described as the branch of the law which:

  1. regulates the relations between citizens and the state
  2. regulates the operations of the various organs of the state

Public law consists of constitutional law, administrative law and criminal law.

Constitutional law consists of those rules which regulate the relationship between different organs of state. These organs of state are the Legislature, the Executive, and the Judiciary.

Administrative law is the law which relates to the actual functioning of the executive instruments of the government.

Criminal law consists of wrongs against the state.

Private Law

This is the branch of law which is primarily concerned with rights and duties of persons towards persons. It governs the relations of the citizens amongst themselves in ordinary transactions. Example of private law is contract law, law of property, law of succession, law of trust e.t.c.

Law as defined above must be distinguished from morality. Morality is the sense of judgment between wrong and right by reference to certain standards developed by society over time.

It consists of standards of behavior widely used by a society (prescriptions of society) and is developed by society. The society determines what is wrong or right to shape the behavior of its members.

Differences between law and morality
# Law Morality
1 Rules of law may be enforced by an action in courts. Morality does not attract the sanction of court for its enforcement unless it also forms part of the law.
2 Rules of law are definite or certain. This means rules of law are easily ascertainable. Moral laws are indefinite and uncertain. They are not easily ascertained.
3 Laws have a general application. Moral laws vary from community to community.
4 Changes faster since it must consistently adapt to social-economic political changes. Does not change frequently and many moral rules have been in existence for a long period of time.
5 Force and the fear of punishment are the main reasons for obedience of law. Conscience is the main reason for being morally upright.
6 These are rules made and enforced by the machinery of government. These are rules made by man concerning the community.
Substantive Law

This consists of actual rules, which generally define or specify what is lawful or unlawful to do. It defines civil and criminal wrongs and provides remedies for each type of offence or civil wrong.

Therefore substantive law defines rights and duties of parties and provides remedies when those rights are violated e.g. law of contract, Negligence, Defamation e.t.c.It also defines offences and prescribes punishment e.g. Penal Code 63.

Procedural Law

Procedural law is also referred to as Adjective Law. It consists of the rule which determines the manner in which the court proceedings are required to be conducted in both civil and criminal cases.

It also consists of legal rules which, defines or specify the steps to be taken or the procedure to be followed by a person who intends to do a lawful act.

The rule which constitutes the procedural law of Kenya are contained in the Civil Procedure Code and the Criminal Procedure Code.
